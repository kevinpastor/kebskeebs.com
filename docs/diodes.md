# 1. Diodes

![SMD diodes, bottom right pad pre-tinned](../assets/01-diodes-pretinned.jpg)
![SMD diodes placed on the PCB](../assets/01-diodes-placed.jpg)
![SMD diodes soldered](../assets/01-diodes-soldered.jpg)

The 35 SMD diodes (1N4148W) go on the **back side** of the PCB.

**Diodes are polarized and orientation matters.** The line on the diode body must match the line marking on the PCB silkscreen. Getting this wrong will cause keys not to register.

1. Pre-tin the bottom right pad of every diode footprint by applying a small amount of solder to it. This is the pad you will use to hold the diode in place.
2. Hold a diode with tweezers in your left hand. Reheat the pre-tinned pad with the iron in your right hand, then slide the diode into position. The line on the diode body must align with the line on the PCB silkscreen.
3. Let the solder cool, then solder the second (left) pad.
4. Reflow the first pad to make sure it has a clean joint.
5. Work through all 35 diodes.

> **Verify:** Look at each diode from above. The line on the diode body should line up with the line on the silkscreen. A phone camera can help for a closer look.

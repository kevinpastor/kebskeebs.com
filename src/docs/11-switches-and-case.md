# 11. Switches, Switch Plate & Case Assembly

The switch plate, PCB, and bottom plate are all held together by the same standoffs, so this all gets done in one go.

![Standoffs screwed into the switch plate](../assets/11-standoffs-in-plate.jpg)
![Switches inserted into the switch plate](../assets/11-switches-in-plate.jpg)
![Switch plate assembly placed on the PCB](../assets/11-switches-on-pcb.jpg)
![Bottom plate attached](../assets/11-bottom-plate.jpg)

1. Screw the four **M2 x 7 mm standoffs** into the **top side of the switch plate** using four **M2 x 5 mm screws**.
2. Insert all 35 switches into the switch plate. **MX switches: the pins face up** (toward the top of the plate).
3. Make sure all switch pins are straight. Bent pins will not seat into the hotswap sockets and can damage them.
4. Carefully lower the plate and switch assembly onto the PCB, lining up the switch pins with the hotswap sockets.
5. Press down firmly and evenly on each switch until it is fully seated. Each switch should sit flush against the PCB with no gaps.
6. Place the bottom plate against the underside of the standoffs and fasten it with the remaining four **M2 x 5 mm screws**.

> **Tip:** Press switches near the corners first to get the plate aligned, then work your way inward.

> **Tip:** Don't overtighten the screws. Snug is enough. Overtightening can crack PCB-based plates.

> **Verify:** None of the switches should wobble. The plate and PCB should sit flush with no gaps.
